Is Metacognition a Better predictor than IQ for Academic Achievement?

A Methodological and Psychometric Critique of Gomes, Golino & Menezes (2014), Psychology, 5, 1095–1110

July 6th 2026

A few days ago, I came across this article making some wild claims about the predictive power of "metacognition". From a quick glance, their number seemed wildly off, so I decided to dig deeper, and the more I dug, the worse it got. Given that their paper has been cited almost 150 times, I decided to compile my findings and make them publicly available.

P.S. It's now July 15th 2026; I've scrutinized my work and the original paper more attentively, and found a couple of minor issues with my critique, but the main points stand. I also found a few greater issues with the original paper I'm critiquing, and the main proof of that would require to re-derive the original correlation matrix. I know this task is feasible because model fit was very high so it should be possible to work backwards from their result, however I lack the necessary knowledge on SEM to do this alone. I may update my critique if I find someone who can help me rederive their matrix, or I learn to do it myself.
